Welcome to the Future of Music Mentorship Portal

This is the central portal for the Future of Music Mentorship Program, supporting youth ages 13–18 in St. Louis through entrepreneurship, academics, and personal growth.

Submit Weekly Goals Schedule Weekly Check-In

Program Overview

Purpose: Support youth ages 13–18 in entrepreneurship, academics, and personal growth.

Mission Statement: To empower youth in St. Louis to build leadership and business skills through peer mentorship and real-world experience.

Target Audience: Youth ages 13–18 in the St. Louis region.

Program Types

  • Entrepreneurship Mentorship
  • Academic Mentorship
  • General / Personal Development Mentorship

Format

  • 1-on-1 mentorship (Virtual / Phone)
  • Weekly meetings
  • 3-month program

Mentors

  • Executive Director (Aereon): All-round mentor
  • Managing Director (Qwentin): All-round mentor
  • Program Coach (Gabriel): Academic & Personal Development

Personal Development

WeekFocus TopicKey Conversation PointsMentor EmphasisGrowth Goal
1Building Self-ConfidenceAsk what’s been going well; highlight strengths and progress.Encourage them with praise but challenge: “You’re improving, but you can go further.”Move from “somewhat confident” to “more self-assured.”
2Taking InitiativeAsk about a time they took action on their own.Reinforce that small steps show leadership. Push gently.Encourage consistent independence.
3Handling FeedbackTalk about a time they received feedback.Remind them feedback = growth.Become comfortable accepting and using critique.
4Networking & CommunicationDiscuss comfort level meeting new people.Role-play small social interactions or give encouragement.Build confidence in social and networking settings.
5Reflection & GrowthAsk how they’ve changed since week 1.Highlight improvement.Develop consistent self-reflection habits.
6Personal LeadershipReflect on how they now lead themselves or others.Reinforce accountability.Solidify self-leadership and goal-driven mindset.

Entrepreneurship

WeekFocus TopicKey Conversation PointsMentor EmphasisGrowth Goal
1Idea Brainstorming“What kind of business would you want to start?”Be affirming but challenging.Build creative thinking and confidence.
2Problem Solving / Target MarketHelp identify customer need.Push for clarity: “Who would buy it? Why you?”Strengthen market awareness.
3Budgeting & Startup CostsGuide through what they’d need to start.Challenge them: “Be smart with your plan.”Develop financial thinking.
4Branding & PromotionAsk how they’d spread the word.Inspire creativity.Build marketing and presentation skills.
5Customer Service & GrowthTalk about customer experience.Refine approach.Learn empathy and problem-solving for clients.
6Pitch & ConfidenceHave them explain their idea fully.Praise growth.Achieve full entrepreneurial confidence.

Academic

WeekFocus TopicKey Conversation PointsMentor EmphasisGrowth Goal
1Study Habits & OrganizationAsk how they manage homework and deadlines.Encourage structure: consistent habits.Build a consistent routine for schoolwork.
2Time ManagementDiscuss how they prioritize tasks.Emphasize planning and efficiency.Balance school, mentorship, and personal time.
3Note-Taking & Learning StylesAsk about methods they use to retain information.Guide them to identify effective techniques.Enhance learning efficiency.
4Critical Thinking & Problem SolvingReview a recent assignment or challenge.Push independent analysis.Develop analytical thinking skills.
5Test Preparation & Study StrategiesAsk how they prepare for tests.Offer tips and strategies.Achieve more confident and effective study habits.
6Reflection & Academic GoalsReflect on growth since week 1.Encourage goal-setting.Track progress consistently.
